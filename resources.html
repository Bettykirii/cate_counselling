Resources

As I do not offer a 24 hour service, if you are feeling a need to urgently speak to someone, the following places are available. You will be listened to about anything you are able to share or you can just be silent on the phone and have the comfort of someone being there with you. These are UK based. Samaritans – Call 116 123 for free “We're waiting for your call. Whatever you're going through, a Samaritan will face it with you.

We're here 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.” HOPELINEUK –Phone: 0800 068 4141 “If you are having thoughts of suicide or are concerned for a young person who might be you can contact HOPELINEUK for confidential support and practical advice.” Text: 07786209697 Email: pat@papyrus-uk.org Opening hours: 10am – 10pm weekdays; 2pm – 10pm weekends and bank holidays

Switchboard – Phone: 0300 330 0630 “The LGBT+ helpline – a place for calm words when you need them most. We’re here to help you with whatever you want to talk about. Nothing is off limits, and we understand how anxious you might feel before you pick up the phone.” – 10am-10pm every day. The Calm Zone – UK Nationwide 0800 58 58 58. London 0808 802 58 58 Campaign Against Living Miserably. Site offering support to men in the UK, of any age, who are down or in crisis via their helpline and website. The lines are open 5pm–midnight, 365 days a year:. They also offer support to those bereaved by suicide, through the Support After Suicide Partnership (SASP). http://supportaftersuicide.org.uk/
