Areas of Interest

I am happy to work with anyone... The isolation and distress that comes from being “different”, not fitting in, not quite like others, being “othered”, “never good enough”, the feeling of being unseen and unheard, labeled.

The consequences of poverty and deprived childhoods, responsibilities beyond people’s capabilities, missed childhoods as a result of “growing up fast” to care for and look after others People with learning difficulties and their carers, disabled people,

LGBTQ+ and in particular from Black and Minority Ethnic backgrounds struggling with the intersectionality of race, sexuality and gender.

The impact of immigration whether that be voluntary or forced by economic, social, ethical or political reasons. According to Nabi (2014), studies indicate that immigrants are probably more affected by mental disorder than the native inhabitants. Leaving behind family, friends and all that people hold close and dear into a new environment, one that may be significantly different to what we know in terms of values, norms, language can have a huge impact on our well being.
