Areas of Interest

The isolation and distress that comes from being “different”, not fitting in, not quite like others, being “othered”, “never good enough”, the feeling of being unseen and unheard, labeled. The consequences of poverty and deprived childhoods, responsibilities beyond your capabilities, missed childhoods because you had to “grow up fast” and care for and look after others People with Learning difficulties and their carers, disabled people, LGBTQ+ and in particular from Black and Minority Ethnic backgrounds struggling with the intersectionality of race, sexuality and gender.
